The IITM-ECR Hub

“Built right, from the ground up.​”

  1. Background and Rationale


    Earth system science is advancing rapidly with the integration of high-resolution models, diverse observational datasets, AI, and interdisciplinary approaches. However, Early Career Researchers (ECRs) often face challenges navigating through the breadth and complexity of the field. ECRs come from various educational backgrounds or domains, each bringing valuable insights, but also facing a steep learning curve when crossing into climate-specific methodologies and frameworks. Recognizing the need for support and collaborative opportunities, we propose the establishment of an IITM-ECR Hub. IITM-ECR Hub can provide structured onboarding, cross-training, and exposure to key climate science concepts and tools. This hub will provide a platform to ECRs for regular discussions, handholding, and a platform for collaborative earth-system related activities such as modeling, prediction, variability, applications, and outreach.
    This initiative will create a vibrant community of young scientists and promote shared learning, collaboration, and innovation. Diverse perspectives from the members of IITM-ECR Hub bring creative problem-solving and modern-day approaches, from AI-driven modelling to community-based development strategies at IITM. While global programs such as WCRP, WWRP, and WMO have dedicated platforms and leadership tracks for ECRs, a similar structured ecosystem is currently lacking at IITM. Hence, the establishment of an ECR hub at IITM will help build a strong, well-supported, and visible ECR community locally, while also establishing active linkages with international ECR networks. The ECR hub will be designed for future expansion across other MoES institutes, fostering a nationally integrated community of early-career scientists.

  2. Who is an ECR


    An Early Career Researcher (ECR) is an individual who is in the early stages of their research career. This typically includes:​

    • Graduate students (Master’s or PhD level)​
    • Postdoctoral researchers
    • Researchers who have completed their highest degree (usually a PhD) within the past 10 years and are actively engaged in research related to climate, atmospheric, oceanic, cryospheric, or broader Earth system sciences.
    • The term is commonly used to recognize and support those who are building their academic and professional profiles, developing independent research agendas, and contributing to their respective fields through scholarly work.​

  3. Objectives


    The ECR Club will aim for:

    1. Professional Development: Empower ECRs with skills in science communication, build professional network, web presence, proposal writing, and portfolio building using open-source tools.
    2. Promote Data and Code Sharing and enhance transparency and reproducibility in climate research via GitHub and other open-source platforms.
    3. Support Interdisciplinary Collaboration and help resolve deadlocks in cross-domain studies.
    4. Create Best Practices & Protocols for modeling workflows that are scalable and accessible.
    5. Build a Bridge to Next-Gen Researchers through online platforms and global collaboration.
    6. Facilitate Knowledge Exchange through regular meetings, seminars, and newsletters.
